Surfing Tips: Spring Tides vs. Neap Tides

Spring Tides

What Are They? Spring tides occur during the new moon and full moon phases, causing the highest high tides and lowest low tides.

Impact on Surfing:

Pros: Stronger, more powerful waves, ideal for experienced surfers.

Cons: Low tides can expose hazards like rocks and reefs. Certain spots may be too shallow.

Neap Tides

What Are They? Neap tides occur during the first quarter and last quarter moon phases, resulting in more moderate high and low tides.

Impact on Surfing:

Pros: Stable conditions, smaller and more predictable waves, ideal for beginners.

Cons: Waves may lack power, especially at spots that rely on strong tidal flow.

Which Is Better?

Spring Tides: Great for experienced surfers seeking bigger, more powerful waves.

Neap Tides: Perfect for beginners or those who prefer gentler, more predictable surf conditions.

Additional Considerations:

Local Knowledge: Certain surf spots perform better at specific tides.

Timing: The best surf conditions often occur during incoming or outgoing tides, depending on the spot.
